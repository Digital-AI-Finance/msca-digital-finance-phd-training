\section{The Four Pillars}
\label{sec:four-pillars}

The Digital Finance training program is organized around four complementary
pillars, each designed to equip doctoral candidates with a distinct set of
competencies.  Together, the pillars ensure that researchers graduate with
deep technical expertise, broad transferable skills, and substantial hands-on
experience in both academic and industry settings.

\textbf{Pillar~1 --- Mandatory Scientific Training.}\quad
Six foundational courses (24~ECTS total) provide every doctoral candidate with
a shared methodological baseline in data science, AI, explainability,
blockchain, sustainable finance, and ethics.  These courses are detailed in
Chapter~\ref{ch:mandatory-courses}.

\textbf{Pillar~2 --- Advanced Scientific Training.}\quad
Eighteen elective courses allow researchers to deepen their expertise in
specialized topics aligned with their work package and thesis.  Offerings span
AI methods, financial modeling, data engineering, policy, and sustainability.
See Chapter~\ref{ch:elective-courses}.

\textbf{Pillar~3 --- Transferable Skills.}\quad
Sixteen non-credit modules develop competencies that extend beyond the research
domain: scientific writing, project management, entrepreneurship, presentation
skills, and research ethics.  See Chapter~\ref{ch:transferable-skills}.

\textbf{Pillar~4 --- Secondments.}\quad
Each doctoral candidate undertakes international secondments at research centers
and industry partners, gaining practical experience and cross-sector exposure.
See Chapter~\ref{ch:secondments}.
